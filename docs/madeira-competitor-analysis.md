## Madeira Market Dynamics

### Why now (2026)?

1. **Post-COVID recovery:** Hotels reopening, upgrading security
2. **Labor costs rising:** Security guards +8% salary inflation (attracting away)
3. **Tourism growth:** More guests = higher theft/security concerns
4. **Tech adoption:** Gen Z entering workforce (expect cloud/mobile)
5. **Portuguese compliance:** CNPD/NIS2 requirements driving digitization

### Customer Segments & Messaging

**Segment 1: Hotels (5-4 star) — 60 properties**
- Pain: Key management across 200+ rooms, staff turnover
- Value: Reduce key card reissue cost (EUR 10 per card × 100s/year)
- Pitch: "Cut key management time by 80%; improve guest security; integrate with your booking system"
- Price sweet spot: EUR 50-70k/year (typical hotel: 150-250 rooms)
- Decision maker: Operations Manager or Security Director
- Timeline: Usually budget cycle Q1-Q2; decision 4-6 weeks

**Segment 2: Senior Living (15 facilities)**
- Pain: Residents wandering, medication security, staff accountability
- Value: Reduce incidents; comply with elder care regulations
- Pitch: "Real-time room access logs for compliance + safety; reduce incident response time"
- Price sweet spot: EUR 40-60k/year
- Decision maker: Care Manager or Administrator
- Timeline: Budget-driven (annual); decision 6-8 weeks

**Segment 3: Private Schools (8 institutions)**
- Pain: School security (unauthorized entry), visitor management, class access
- Value: Parent peace-of-mind; incident documentation for insurance
- Pitch: "Secure classroom access; real-time alerts for unauthorized entry; parent app notifications"
- Price sweet spot: EUR 30-50k/year (smaller than hotels)
- Decision maker: Principal or Business Manager
- Timeline: Usually budget summer (for Sep reopening); decision 8-10 weeks

**Segment 4: Corporate Offices (25 sites)**
- Pain: High turnover (IT onboarding), confidentiality (office access), meeting room availability
- Value: Reduce onboarding time (from 2 days to 2 hours); improve space utilization
- Pitch: "Automated office access for new hires; meeting room reservation integration; usage analytics"
- Price sweet spot: EUR 60-80k/year (larger sites, more complexity)
- Decision maker: CISO or Facilities Manager
- Timeline: Fiscal year planning (often Q3-Q4); decision 6-8 weeks

## Sales Strategy vs Competitors

### Why customers choose Securitas
- Brand (assumed reliable)
- Local office (trusted relationship)
- 24/7 support

**Counter-tactics:**
- Offer SLA with same 24h response (documented)
- Emphasize **transparency** (no hidden fees, clear pricing)
- Get early customer testimonials (one hotel's "we saved EUR 20k" beats Securitas' marketing)
- Partner with local consultancies (build local presence without overhead)

### Why customers choose Prosegur
- Existing relationship (they already provide security guards)
- Bundle deal (access + guards = "integrated solution")

**Counter-tactics:**
- Position as **complement, not replacement** (keep their guards; our system improves guard efficiency)
- Offer integration (our API + their CCTV / alarm system = unified dashboard)
- Target hotels Prosegur doesn't serve well (smaller, budget-conscious)

### Why customers choose DIY
- Cost (don't want to spend EUR 50k)
- Think they don't need it (false economy)

**Counter-tactics:**
- Financing option (EUR 50k/year = EUR 4k/month; position as OPEX not CAPEX)
- TCO analysis (if current system: manual key management costs EUR 15k/year in labor, our system costs EUR 50k but saves EUR 25k/year = breakeven in 2-3 years)
- Free pilot (install system in one building for 2 weeks, prove ROI)
